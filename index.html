Wings of Heritage: The PAF Story

Foundations of Valor

The Philippine Air Force traces its lineage to the Philippine Constabulary Air Corps (PCAC) formed in the early 1930s. Formally established as a separate branch of the AFP in 1947, the PAF inherited a legacy of courage and technical skill that laid the groundwork for the defense of our sovereign airspace.

Guardians of the Skies

Throughout the decades, the PAF played a vital role in national security and regional stability. From the iconic P-51 Mustangs of the post-war era to the supersonic F-5 Freedom Fighters, the Air Force has consistently evolved to meet the challenges of each generation, earning the respect of its neighbors and the trust of its people.

Preserving Aviation Heritage

The PAF Aerospace Museum at Col. Jesus Villamor Air Base was established to immortalize the stories of our airmen. It houses an impressive collection of aircraft, uniforms, weapons, and equipment, serving as a sanctuary of knowledge for students, military historians, and aviation enthusiasts alike.

Inspiring Next-Gen Aviators

Moving forward, the museum is embracing digital transformation. Through interactive exhibits and Augmented Reality (AR) technology, we aim to make history tangible for the youth. Our goal is to ignite the flame of patriotism and inspire the next generation of Filipino pilots, engineers, and aerospace leaders.

Philippine Air Force Aerospace Museum

Serving as a beacon of aeronautical history, the PAF Aerospace Museum is dedicated to honoring the legacy of Filipino airmen and educating the public on the vital role of the Air Force in national security.
